# Decision Boundary Visualization - Ethical Considerations and Limitations

## Research and Educational Use Only

This project is designed exclusively for research and educational purposes. The decision boundary visualizations and explanations provided are:

- **Experimental**: Methods may produce unstable or inconsistent results
- **Approximations**: Visualizations represent simplified 2D projections of complex high-dimensional spaces
- **Context-dependent**: Results may vary significantly across different datasets, models, and configurations

## Important Limitations

### Technical Limitations
- Decision boundaries are computed on 2D projections and may not reflect true model behavior
- High-dimensional decision surfaces cannot be fully captured in 2D visualizations
- Boundary stability may vary across different random seeds and data splits
- Computational complexity limits real-time visualization for large datasets

### Interpretability Limitations
- Visualizations may be misleading if the underlying model is biased or poorly trained
- Decision boundaries do not explain the reasoning process behind individual predictions
- Feature interactions and non-linear relationships may be obscured in 2D projections
- Boundary visualizations cannot detect adversarial vulnerabilities or robustness issues

### Ethical Considerations
- **No Medical/Financial Decisions**: Never use these visualizations for medical diagnosis, financial advice, or other high-stakes decisions
- **Human Oversight Required**: All interpretations should be validated by domain experts
- **Bias Detection**: Visualizations may not reveal subtle biases present in the underlying model
- **Privacy**: Ensure no personally identifiable information is included in visualizations

## Recommendations for Responsible Use

1. **Validate Independently**: Always verify findings through multiple methods and expert review
2. **Document Limitations**: Clearly state the scope and limitations of any visualizations used
3. **Consider Context**: Interpret results within the specific domain and use case context
4. **Regular Updates**: Keep methods and evaluations current with latest research
5. **Transparency**: Document all assumptions, preprocessing steps, and methodological choices

## Contact and Support

For questions about ethical use or technical limitations, please refer to the project documentation or create an issue in the repository.

**Remember**: These tools are aids to understanding, not substitutes for human judgment and domain expertise.
